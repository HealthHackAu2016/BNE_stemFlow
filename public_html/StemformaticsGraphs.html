Scatter Graph

The scatter graph has a different structure than the other two graph types. Here the samples are on the x axis and the colours represent the probes.

  1. Points are normally individual readings from the given probe and sample.
  2. Where technical replicates are available however, the points are an average of the replicates with the bars representing their standard deviation.

You can mouse over the points on the graph to show their sample id (also called replicate group ID).

The black values at the bottom of the graph are the Sample Types for this dataset.

The green and blue lines are the dataset's median expression value and detection threshold respectively.

NextPrevious

Bar Graph

The bar graph displays the expression data of the gene's probes grouped by the Sample Type.

  1. Mean expression value for the probe and Sample Type.
  2. Error bars representing +/- 1 standard deviation.

The values at the bottom of the graph are the probes that map to this gene for this dataset's platform. If they are in red the probes multi-map to other genes.

The green and blue lines are the dataset's median expression value and detection threshold respectively.

NextPrevious

Boxplot Graph

The default graph type is a box plot. It displays the expression data of the gene's probes grouped by the Sample Type.

  1. Probe and Sample Type's maximum expression value.
  2. 3rd quartile.
  3. Probe and Sample Type's median expression value.
  4. 1st quartile.
  5. Probe and Sample Type's minimum expression value.

The values at the bottom of the graph are the probes that map to this gene for this dataset's platform. If they are in red the probes multi-map to other genes.

The green and blue lines are the dataset's median expression value and detection threshold respectively.

NextPrevious

Line Graph

The line graph displays the expression data grouped by time and a grouping specified by the annotator. The members of the group are then connected by a line across the time x-axis using a unique colour. Normally, multiple Sample Types might be grouped together in the Line Graph Group to show the overall profile by time. Probes at the bottom are red if they multi-map to other genes

  1. Error bars representing +/- 1 standard deviation as two samples of the same Line Graph Group are at the same time point eg. Two hESC samples at Day 0.
  2. Mean expression value for the aggregated probe, Line Graph Group and time
  3. Line Graph Group over time that has the one colour and multiple samples per timepoint.

The green and blue lines are the dataset's median expression value and detection threshold respectively.
